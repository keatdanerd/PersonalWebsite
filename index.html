About Me

Hi, I'm Keaton

I'm a chemistry undergraduate at Abilene Christian University with experience in research and leadership. My interests span the sciences and the humanities, from physical chemistry and biology to history and religion.

Outside the lab, I serve in student government, lead Chem Club as the chaplain, and mentor underclassmen. I believe in lifelong learning and hope to impact the world through science and intentional relationships.

Experience

Here are some of my recent positions and research experiences.

Protein Research

Advanced the study on the multimeric states of mvaE fusion protein found in the mevalonate pathway using vector plasmid manipulation and protein expression techniques.

Caltech WAVE Fellow

Designed an experiment to study carbon radical recombination using pulse laser photolysis cavity ring-down spectroscopy.

Microbiology Research

Studied quorum sensing inhibition and the biosynthetic pathway of prodigiosin in S. Marcescens.

Teaching Assistant and Tutor

Developed individualized tutoring sessions in chemistry and biology courses. Taught organic chemistry wet lab and spectroscopy techniques to undergraduates at ACU.

Student Government

Elected as the sophomore class president and as a class senator to serve the student body. Worked in the student affairs and sustainability committees.

Inorganic Chemistry Research

Synthesized novel organometallics to study hydrogenase mimics, with a focus on osmium containing compounds.
